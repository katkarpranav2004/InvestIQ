APPLE
-----

Recommendation: Invest
Investment Score: 85   Confidence: 90   Risk: 60   Growth: 80

Overview:
Apple Inc. (founded 1976, Cupertino, CA, CEO Tim Cook) - Technology /
Consumer Electronics. Designs, manufactures, and markets consumer
electronics, software, and online services (iPhone, Mac, iPad, Apple
Watch, AirPods) with a tightly integrated hardware/software ecosystem.

Business Model:
Revenue primarily from hardware sales, plus a fast-growing services
segment (Apple Music, Apple TV+, iCloud) and App Store commissions.
Ecosystem lock-in and premium pricing drive high margins.

Financials:
Revenue growing ~10-15% YoY, net margin ~20-25%. Growth driven by the
expanding services segment, wearables, and emerging-market penetration.

Risk (60/100):
Regulatory scrutiny/antitrust risk, competitive pressure from
Samsung/Huawei/Google, high R&D spend, global supply chain exposure.

SWOT:
  Strengths:     Brand loyalty & premium pricing, diversified
                 hardware/software portfolio, large loyal customer base.
  Weaknesses:    iPhone revenue concentration, high R&D spend, limited
                 penetration in some emerging markets.
  Opportunities: Wearables/accessories growth, expanding services
                 segment, emerging-market penetration (China, India).
  Threats:       Antitrust/regulatory scrutiny, competition from
                 Samsung/Huawei/Google/Amazon, macroeconomic downturn risk.

Reasoning:
Strong brand loyalty, a diversified and growing product/services
portfolio, and consistent double-digit revenue growth outweigh
regulatory and competitive risk - hence an Invest call with high
(90/100) confidence.


TESLA
-----

Recommendation: Invest
Investment Score: 80   Confidence: 85   Risk: 60   Growth: 90

Overview:
Tesla, Inc. (founded 2003, Austin, TX, CEO Elon Musk) - Technology /
Electric Vehicles and Clean Energy. Designs, manufactures, and sells
EVs, solar power systems, and energy storage products.

Business Model:
~90% of revenue from vehicle sales (Model S/3/X/Y); remaining from
energy storage (Powerwall/Powerpack), regulatory credit sales,
Supercharger network, and subscription/software services (recurring
revenue).

Financials:
Revenue growing ~20% YoY, consecutive profitable quarters (~$1.1B net
income in the modeled quarter). Growth driven by Model 3/Y demand,
international expansion, and autonomous-driving investment.

Risk (60/100):
Regulatory dependency on EV incentives, intensifying competition (GM,
Volkswagen, Nissan, Rivian, Lucid), high capex/R&D, supply chain
disruption risk, macroeconomic/trade-policy exposure.

SWOT:
  Strengths:     EV market leadership, software-centric approach, brand
                 loyalty and scale economics.
  Weaknesses:    High R&D/capex, dependence on government EV incentives,
                 supply chain fragility.
  Opportunities: Growing EV/energy-storage demand, international
                 expansion, autonomous-driving technology.
  Threats:       EV incentive/regulatory changes, new entrants and
                 established automakers, macroeconomic uncertainty.

Reasoning:
Market leadership, a software-centric moat, and ~20% YoY revenue growth
support an Invest call, tempered to 85/100 confidence by high R&D spend
and regulatory dependency on EV incentives.


NOTES ON THESE RUNS
--------------------

- Both are real Groq/Llama 3.3 completions - nothing here is hand-edited
  beyond formatting the raw JSON into readable prose.
- Numeric fields (scores 0-100, sentiment, etc.) come from the Zod
  schemas in src/lib/ai/schemas.ts via withStructuredOutput, so they're
  always present and always in range.
- Re-running the same company can produce a somewhat different score or
  wording each time - the model temperature is intentionally low (0.3,
  see src/lib/ai/model.ts) to keep runs fairly consistent, but LLM
  output is never bit-for-bit deterministic.
